**Body:**

Not a toy. Not a gadget. A shared digital pet that lives in the office and needs the team to survive. When it's lonely, it fades. When the office gets too loud, it gets stressed. Someone has to take it for a walk — with a colleague.

The result? In 30 days:
- 47 "healing walks" taken in pairs
- Office noise complaints dropped 60%
- Someone actually said "good morning" without being prompted

The problem isn't that people don't want to connect. It's that we forgot how to create natural excuses for it.

A robot cat is a ridiculous solution to a serious problem. But it works.

## Post 4: Case Study

**Body:**

The Intervention:
Placed Office Cat — a shared digital pet with health, mood, and social needs — in the kitchen area. No training. No mandate. Just a pet that needs the team.
