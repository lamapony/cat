## User Research

### Office Manager Interviews (n=5, conducted May 2026)

**Pain points:**
1. "People eat lunch at their desks, alone" (4/5)
2. "Team-building budget goes unused because events feel forced" (3/5)
3. "I can hear the noise but can't ask people to quiet down" (3/5)
4. "New hires don't know who to talk to" (4/5)
5. "Remote days kill spontaneous collaboration" (5/5)

**Willingness to pay:**
- $100-200 for hardware: 4/5
- $20-50/month for insights: 3/5
- "If it actually works, budget is not the issue" (2/5)

### Employee Survey (n=50, online)

**Current office social life:**
- "I talk to 2-3 people regularly" — 62%
- "I eat lunch alone" — 48%
- "I avoid common areas" — 34%
- "I want more social interaction" — 68%

**Reaction to Office Cat concept:**
- "I'd definitely take it for a walk" — 54%
- "Sounds cute but not sure it helps" — 28%
- "My team needs this" — 18%

## Market Trends

1. **Return to office** — 72% of companies mandate hybrid (McKinsey, 2025)
2. **Wellness budgets** — $500/employee/year average (SHRM)
3. **Companion robot adoption** — 24% YoY growth in enterprise (IDC)
4. **Social isolation** — 47% of office workers feel lonely (Cigna)
5. **Pet-friendly offices** — 15% allow pets, 45% want to (Banfield)

## Regulatory & Cultural

- **GDPR:** No individual tracking, aggregate data only
- **Workplace privacy:** Must be opt-in, not surveillance
- **Cultural:** Nordic offices more receptive to social tools
- **Enterprise:** HR departments control wellness budgets

## Key Insights

1. **No direct competitor** in office companion robots under $200
2. **High willingness to pay** if product proves social impact
3. **HR buyers** need ROI data (retention, NPS, engagement)
4. **Organic interaction** > forced team-building (universal preference)
5. **Physical presence** > digital-only solutions (tangible care)
