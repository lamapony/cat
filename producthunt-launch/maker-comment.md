Hey ProductHunt 👋

Six months ago, I walked into my office and realized something weird: 40 people, dead silence. Everyone had headphones on. Nobody was eating lunch together. The "team" was just 40 individuals sharing a WiFi password.

We tried the usual stuff — team-building exercises, Slack channels, even a foosball table. Nothing stuck. People checked their phones through "bonding activities." The foosball table became a coat rack.

Then one evening, watching my actual cat demand attention, I had a stupid idea: **What if the office had a pet that needed the team to survive?**

Not a productivity tool. Not a surveillance device. Just a reason to talk to each other.

**Office Cat** was born.

---

## What it actually does

It's a small robot cat that sits on your desk. It has needs — it gets lonely, stressed by noise, bored. When it starts fading (grey screen, droopy ears), someone notices and says: *"Hey, the cat looks sad. Want to take it for a walk?"*

Two people walk for 10 minutes. The cat heals. They talk. That's it.

No forced fun. No corporate awkwardness. Just a pet that creates natural excuses for human connection.

---

## The build

We built the first prototype in 24 hours at a hackathon using the Atech.dev Big Kit ($99). Four people. One day. One robot cat.

The hardware is simple: noise sensor, touch sensor, motion sensor, LED matrix for the face, speaker for sounds, WiFi connectivity. The magic is in the behavior design — the cat's emotional state mirrors what the team actually needs.
